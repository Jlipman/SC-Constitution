Student Government Constitution
(Revised September 24, 2003)

Article 1

Section 1: This constitution shall be printed every year in the Student handbook. In addition, it will be read aloud, in full, at the first meeting of the new session of Student Government.

Section 2: This constitution shale reviewed and possibly revised at the end of each year before the election of new officers. This will ensure that this constitution is continually fulfilling the needs of Student Government.

Article 2

The Student Government of the University of Chicago Laboratory High School shall be composed of the following committees:

  1. The Student Council (SC).
  2. The Cultural Union (CU).
  3. The Executive Committee.
  4. The Senior Class Steering Committee.
  5. The Junior Class Steering Committee.
  6. The Sophomore Class Steering Committee.
  7. The Freshman Class Steering Committee.
  8. Any Ad Hoc Committees established during the school year.
  9. The Course Evaluation Committee.
Article 3

All the members of Student Government shall be elected by the appropriate members of the student body to represent them in all functions deemed necessary. Elections shall be planned and administered by the Executive Committee.

Article 4

  • Section 1: The purpose of the Student Council is to:
    • represent student interests pertinent political issues
    • control the allocations of Student Council funds
  • Section 2: Student Council members should:
    • actively seek suggestions and concerns of the student body
    • serve as leaders in the school community by setting an example of good character and behavior
    • cultivate school spirit by attending school activities and encouraging other students to attend these activities
  • Section 3: Student Council shall be composed of the following officers:
    • President
    • Vice-President
    • Secretary
    • Treasurer
    • President of the Cultural Union
    • Treasurer of the Cultural Union
    • President of each class
    • One Vice-President from each class
    • Two Cultural Union representatives
  • Section 4: Duties of the Executive Committee Officers:
    1. The President of Student Council shall preside at all meetings of Student Council; shall prepare a written agenda; shall be an ex-officio member of all Student Council Committees; shall appoint all members (including the Chairman) of all Ad Hoc Committees.
    2. The Executive Vice-President shall perform all duties of the President in the the President’s absence.
    3. The Treasurer shall keep all accounts of Student Council and review those of other Student Council subsidized organizations.
    4. The Executive Committee shall write an agenda each week for the upcoming ALL-School meetings; shall maintain communication with the Cultural Union; shall monitor weekly steering committee meetings; shall handle impeachment proceedings.

Article 5

Section 1: The purpose of this organization shall be to plan and administer all-school social and cultural activities that are not related to club activities.

Section 2: The Cultural Union shall be composed of the Cultural Union President, the Cultural Union Treasurer, and the two Cultural Union Representatives form each class.

Section 3: Duties of the Officers

  1. The Cultural Union President shall provide at Cultural Union meetings; shall attend Student Council meetings every other week; shall oversee the organization and administration of all-school activities; shall attend all Student Council executive meetings.
  2. The Cultural Union Treasurer shall be aware of activities that Cultural Union plans; shall be responsible for finding out costs of items needed for activities; shall keep written records of expenditures of Cultural Union and attendance; shall take and post agendas of all Cultural Union meetings; shall attend all Student Council and executive meetings and shall be the advisor to the Cultural Union President.
  3. The Cultural Union Representatives shall transmit ideas from their respective classes to the Cultural Union; shall organize all-school functions; shall be responsible for events sponsored by Cultural Union and shall attend Student Council meetings every other week.
Article 6

Section 1: The purpose of the Steering Committees shall be to plan and administer class social, cultural, and intellectual activities, to represent the class on any of its interests and to write one survey each quarter and post the results of that survey. The Steering Committees shall be responsible for informing students in his/her class of Student Council issues and decisions.

Section 2: The Steering Committee shall be composed of a President, Vice President and two Cultural Union Representatives.

Section 3: Duties of the Officers

  1. The Class President shall attend Student Council meetings; shall represent his/her class’ interests in Student Council; shall organize class activities on the basis of wants of needs of the students in his/her class; shall publicize these events to his/her class; shall be responsible for setting meeting times and places and posting these; shall write a weekly agenda and post it on the Student Council boards and preside over the Steering Committee meetings.
  2. The Vice President shall request money from the student council to fund class activities; shall help to organize and publicize these activities shall maintain the class board; shall attend Student Council meetings.
  3. The Cultural Union Representatives shall be responsible for informing the Steering Committee of Cultural Union-sponsored events; shall report activities planned and problems encountered in planning these class events to the Cultural Union President; shall assist in planning and publicizing all class activities ands shall serve as a link between the Student Council and the Cultural Union.

Section 4: The Steering Committees shall be required to hold at least one class assembly or open forum per quarter in order to find out ideas of the class, to inform the class of accomplishments, and to achieve unity within the class.

Section 5: The Steering Committees shall be required to hold at least one social event per quarter in order to foster class unity and reduce stress.

Article 7
(Omitted due to irrelevance)
Article 8

Section 1: Every committee of Student Council must meet as necessary as specified by the Chairman and the Chairman must report with and agenda with noted decisions to the next meeting of the Student Council. Meeting times must be posted beforehand on the Student Council Board. Ad Hoc Committees may be relieved of their duties when they are dissolved. Permanent and Ad Hoc committees may be relieved of their duties by a majority vote of the Student Council.

Section 2: Any committee decision is subject to approval by Student Council.

Article 9

Section 1: Any member of the Student Council can be impeached by a majority vote of the Student Council with one week’s notice prior to the impeachment process.

Section 2: Grounds for impeachment include failure to fulfill obligations of this constitution and/or lack of concern or enthusiasm in Student Council (which is when a member of Student Council fulfills obligations, but shows no initiative towards a meaningful contribution to Student Council). The Executive Committee has the right to determine whether these obligations are met by relying on a unanimous vote.

Section 3: Impeachment of all-school members and class representatives and officers of Student Council may be initiated by a petition signed by one-fifth of the student body. Upon receiving the signed petition, the Student Council shall review the situation and follow through with the impeachment of an officers if 2/3 of the Student Council votes in favor of that action.

Section 4: Upon impeachment, an election will be held by the Student Council to fill the position. Candidates will present speeches exclusively to Student Council. Student Council will then decide on the new officer through a 2/3 vote. If no one steps forward to fill the office, the President of the Student council will appoint someone to the positions.

Article 10

Each member of the Student Council is required to attend all official meetings of any section of Student Council to which her/she belongs (committees included). Each member is permitted only one (1) unexposed absence per quarter [the only excuse absences being absent from school due to illness or a prearranged absence (a prearranged absence signed by a teacher or parent must be shown to the President or Vice-President)], whereupon the member is on probation (notification is needed). Three unexcused tardies equals one unexcused absence. The next unexcused absence results in an impeachment trial run by the Executive Committee and the Student Council advisor. Someone appointed by the President and approved by the Student Council will fill the vacant spot. If a Student Council member is more than ten minutes late to a meeting, the tardy is considered an unexcused absence.

Article 11

The Secretary of the Student Council will construct and maintain the Student Council Bulletin Board on the second floor upon which are required to be placed:

  • Meeting agendas of each Steering Committee and any sub-committee of the Student Council within two (2) days of the meeting.
  • A calendar that includes meeting times and Student Council events
  • A suggestion box
  • Any other information dealing with Student Council and the student body
Article 12

A two-thirds vote of the total membership of Student Council is required in order to amend the Constitution.

Article 13

Section 1: The Student Government is responsible for running the elections process for members to the discipline board.

Article 14

The student body, having ratified this Constitution, recognizes it as an independent entity belonging to the students, and not subject to interference from individuals or groups outside the student body.

Appendix: Student Council Election Procedures
  1. Any student may nominate him/herself for election to any available office. To do so, he/she must obtain a petition and acquire the set number of signatures from eligible voters. (This means that no out-going Seniors or in-coming Freshmen are allowed to sign petitions in spring elections -- see Rule 3). The nominee must then sign and turn in the petition by the end of the school day, five (5) school days before the election.
  2. Only in-coming Seniors will be eligible to run for Senior Class offices. Similarly, only in-coming Juniors will be eligible to run for Junior Class offices; only in-coming Sophomores will be eligible to run for Sophomore Class offices; only in-coming Freshman will be eligible to run for Freshman Class offices.
  3. All in-coming Sophomores, Juniors, and Seniors shall be eligible to vote in all school elections in the spring. In-coming Freshman and out-going Seniors are not eligible to vote in such elections.
  4. The elections shall be publicly announced at least three weeks before they are to take place. Petitions will be made available no later than two weeks before elections. All petitions for candidacy must be given to the Vice-President of Student Council or brought to U-High 003.
  5. Petitions for All-School positions must be signed by at least sixty (60) members of the student body. Petitions for Class Steering Committee positions of President and of Treasurer must be signed by forty (40) members of that class. Petitions for all other Class Steering Committee positions must be signed by twenty (20) members of that class.
  6. A student may only run for one office. If a student turns in more than one petition for more than one office, he/she will not be considered for candidacy in any office.
  7. A sample ballot, listing candidates for all positions open, shall be posted on the Student Council board three (3) school days before the election.
  8. Any student may run as a write-in candidate if he/she so wishes.
  9. Any students elected against his/her will may turn down the positions, relieving the post to the first runner-up.
  10. There will be no campaigning within 2- feet of the ballot box on the day of the election, or the name of the candidate will automatically be disqualified. Otherwise, campaigning will be permitted and encouraged.
  11. Election judges may not give any information or advice to any voter, nor may any candidate for office serve as an election judge.
  12. All ballots will be initialed by the election judge and immediately placed in the ballot box. The name of the voter shall then be crossed off the voter registration list. If any ballot is found without the proper identification on it, it will be disregarded.
  13. Miscounting ballots, stuffing the ballot box, or falsely identifying someone wishing to obtain a ballot may all result in disciplinary action.
  14. The President, Vice-President, and two Student Council members not running for office shall be present at the counting of the ballots.
  15. Outcome of the elections shall be posted by lunch the school day following the election.
  16. For any office for which there is more than one person running, the person receiving the most votes shall be declared the winner. In the case of a tie, a run-off election shall be held the week following the regular elections.
  17. When there are two people to be elected to the same office (i.e., CU representatives), and more than two are candidates for that office, the two candidates receiving the most votes shall be declared the winners.
  18. Any infringement of these rules are grounds for disciplinary action and possible disqualification where applicable, The extent of such action will be determined by a 2/3 vote of the Student Council.
  19. In order for the candidates to make their views known, Student Council will hold a forum in which candidates for all major offices will give speeches.
Responsibilities of Student Council Member
  • To support the missions of the University of Chicago, the Laboratory Schools, University High School, and the Student Council;
  • To be a model of good citizenship to all University High School Students;
  • To serve as an ambassador of the student body to the community;
  • To keep informed of School events and activities;
  • To make a positive contribution to the Laboratory Schools community by attending School events, offering encouragement and support, and by volunteering to help whenever needed;
  • To be a stanch supporter of the activities of the Student Council by taking an active role in planning, staffing, and publicizing all Student Council events;
  • To attend regularly all the General Meetings and Class Meetings, attend sub-committee meetings as requested, and check for Student Council e-mail daily.
